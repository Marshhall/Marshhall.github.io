I am Physics and Mathematics student at the University of Barcelona. Needless to say, my passion lies in the intersection between these two sciences. I am particularly driven towards the geometric description of physical theories, and also the possible game-changing applications of quantum computing.

One year ago, I joined a research group at CRM that works on complex analysis. Our aim was to study the complex exponential family and trascendental dynamics. It was a great experience to get my first toe into the pool of research.

In fact, I enjoyed it so much that I could not wait to join another group. But this time, I was looking for a more applied and interdisciplinary work. This led me to participate in a summer program at the IRB, where I could apply my knowledge in Physics and Mathematics to solve biology-related problems. I had the pleasure of working in the Molecular Modelling and Bioinformatics group.

My interests are currently in the intersection between Mathematics and Physics, what is known as the field of mathematical physics. Many times, studying one system from the abstract point of view that is offered by mathematics can lead to great progress and advancements that would have otherwhise required formidable efforts to achieve.

However, this is a two-way street, since physics has helped create new mathematics in several occasions. Calculus is a rather old-fashioned example, but more modern physical theoris, like QFT, serve as a fruitful playground for the emergence of new mathematical concepts. I would encourage you to have a look at this Quanta Magazine article if you want more details.

Outside the realm of science, I am absorbed by literature, especially ancient Greek and Roman. However, I also enjoy more recent works.
