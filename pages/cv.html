Research Experience

December 2024 - Present

Research Assistant

Barcelona Supercomputing Center |

Barcelona, Spain

Working on improving quantum learning through classical shadows.

In quantum computing, a major challenge is to extract information from a quantum state. A promising solution, known as "classical shadows", was recently introduced by John Preskill and collaborators. In this project, conducted within the Quantic group at BSC, we are implementing this technique and using it to train quantum algorithms. By employing efficient measurements and a compact representation, we aim to capture the essential properties of a quantum state.

July 2024 - February 2025

Visiting Student

Institute for Research in Biomedicine |

Barcelona, Spain

Analysis of DNA flexibility under damage using a Coarse-Grained model.

This project aimed to develop Coarse-Grained simulations to investigate changes in DNA flexibility under damage. Its goal was to explain previous experimental findings related to skin cancer. This was done using a model previously developed in Professor Modesto Orozco's lab (CGeNArate), which needed to be optimised to account for DNA damage.

During our preliminary study, we devised and implemented new methods to calculate the curvature of DNA in coarse-grained simulations. Then, using constrained stochastic optimisation techniques, we successfully adapted the model to handle a wide variety of sequential damages. The results we obtained are promising, and our improved model can now be used to explore new DNA configurations without the need for expensive experimental tests.
