Research Experience

November 2024 - June 2025

Visiting Student

Barcelona Supercomputing Center |

Barcelona, Spain

Optimized quantum learning using classical shadows and observable grouping.

In quantum computing, a major challenge is to efficiently extract information from a quantum system. During my work within the Quantic group at BSC I developed a hybrid method called Shadow-Grouping, which combines two powerful techniques: classical shadows (for estimating many observables with few measurements) and observable grouping (to reduce redundant measurements).

We first showed that this approach significantly improves measurement efficiency on today's quantum devices. We then demonstrated its effectiveness by estimating the ground state energies of several many-body quantum systems. Notably, Shadow-Grouping achieved up to 18x higher accuracy than standard methods while requiring orders of magnitude fewer measurements.

July 2024 - February 2025

Research Intern

Institute for Research in Biomedicine |

Barcelona, Spain

Analyzed DNA flexibility under damage using a Coarse-Grained model.

This project aimed to develop Coarse-Grained simulations to investigate changes in DNA flexibility under damage. Its goal was to explain previous experimental findings related to skin cancer. This was done using a model (CGeNArate), previously developed at Professor Modesto Orozco's lab, which needed to be optimised to account for DNA damage.

During our preliminary study, we devised and implemented new methods to calculate the curvature of DNA in coarse-grained simulations. Then, using constrained stochastic optimisation techniques, we successfully adapted the model to handle a wide variety of sequential damages. The results we obtained are promising, and our improved model can now be used to explore new DNA configurations without the need for expensive experimental tests.

During my summer internship at the CRM, I studied transcendental dynamics within the complex exponential family.
